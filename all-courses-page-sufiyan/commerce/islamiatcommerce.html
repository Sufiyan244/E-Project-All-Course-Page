Introduction

Islamiat is an important subject taught across various academic fields, including commerce. While commerce focuses on business, finance, management, and market systems, Islamiat plays a unique role by helping students develop strong moral values, ethical behavior, and a responsible approach to decision-making.

In today’s business world, where honesty, transparency, and integrity matter greatly, the teachings of Islam guide commerce students to become fair, trustworthy, and socially responsible professionals. Islamiat strengthens a student’s character and provides them with principles that improve both personal and professional life.

Below are major Islamiat topics, explained in detail, that benefit commerce students.

1. Islamic Business Ethics (Akhlaqiyat in Business)

One of the most important contributions of Islamiat to the commerce field is the concept of Islamic business ethics. Islam emphasizes honesty, fairness, transparency, trust, and fulfilling commitments—qualities that are essential for running ethical businesses.

Key points students learn:

  • Always give fair weight and avoid cheating
  • Fulfill promises made to customers and partners
  • Ensure fairness in buying and selling
  • Avoid fraud, bribery, and deception
  • Treat employees with respect and justice

Why it matters in commerce:

Islamic ethics help prevent corruption, develop customer trust, and create a positive business reputation. A commerce student who understands these values becomes a more responsible entrepreneur or employee.

2. Concept of Halal and Haram in Business

Islamiat teaches students the difference between halal (permissible) and haram (prohibited) earnings. This becomes extremely important in business, banking, marketing, and finance.

Examples of Halal Earnings:

  • Fair trade
  • Honest profit
  • Providing real services
  • Ethical investments

Examples of Haram Earnings:

  • Interest (Riba)
  • Gambling
  • Fraud
  • Selling prohibited items

Benefit for commerce students::

They learn to choose business activities that are lawful, ethical, and beneficial for society.

3. Social Responsibility in Islam (Falah-e-Bahmi)

Islam encourages helping others, supporting communities, and using resources responsibly. This is directly connected to corporate social responsibility (CSR) in modern business.

Islamic teachings related to CSR:

  • Giving charity (Zakat and Sadaqah)
  • Taking care of the poor and needy
  • Not wasting resources
  • Protecting the environment
  • Running businesses that benefit society

Why this matters for commerce students:

They learn to build businesses that not only earn profit but also support community welfare.

4. The Life of the Prophet (Seerat-un-Nabi) as a Model for Business

The life of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) provides countless lessons for business ethics and leadership. He was known for honesty and was titled Al-Ameen (The Trustworthy) long before prophethood.

Lessons commerce students gain:

  • Truthfulness in trade
  • Keeping promises
  • Treating customers with respect
  • Maintaining fairness in partnerships
  • Showing kindness, patience, and good manners

Impact on commerce students:

These qualities help them become respected professionals with strong moral character.

5. Islamic Teachings About Time Management

Time is considered a blessing in Islam, and managing it wisely is encouraged.

What students learn:

  • The importance of planning their daily tasks
  • Avoiding laziness and procrastination
  • Balancing worship, work, and rest
  • Completing tasks with discipline

Impact on commerce students:

Good time management leads to better productivity, meeting deadlines, and higher job performance.

6. Accountability and Responsibility (Hisab & Zimmedari)

Islam teaches that every action will be questioned and judged. This encourages individuals to act responsibly.

Practical benefits:

  • Students make ethical decisions
  • They become honest in financial matters
  • They avoid cheating and dishonesty
  • They develop a responsible mindset for the workplace

Conclusion

Islamiat is not just a religious subject—it is a guide for living a meaningful, ethical, and successful life. For commerce students, it provides principles that shape honesty, justice, fairness, and responsibility in business.

By learning Islamic teachings, students develop strong character, professional discipline, and a commitment to ethical business practices. These values help them become better entrepreneurs, trustworthy employees, and responsible members of society.

In short, Islamiat strengthens both the moral and professional foundation of commerce students, preparing them for a successful future with integrity and faith.
