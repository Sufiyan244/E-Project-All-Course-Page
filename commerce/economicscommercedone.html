Introduction

Economics is a key subject in the First Year Commerce program, giving students a basic understanding of how individuals, firms, and governments make financial decisions. To score well in Karachi Board exams, students should focus on the most important and frequently tested topics.

1. Basic Economic Concepts

The foundation of Economics begins with Basic Economic Concepts such as scarcity, choice, wants, goods, and services. Students must understand these terms clearly because they appear repeatedly in short questions and MCQs.

2. Opportunity Cost

A very important topic is Opportunity Cost. It explains how people choose between different options when resources are limited. This concept helps students understand real-life decision-making and is commonly asked in exams.

3. Law of Demand

The Law of Demand is a major topic that every student should learn. Knowing the schedule, graph, and factors affecting demand is helpful for both short and long questions. This topic is easy and scoring if practiced properly.

4. Law of Supply

Similarly, the Law of Supply explains how producers respond to changes in price. Students should learn the supply curve, its movement, and the factors that influence supply. These questions often come in board exams.

5. National Income

The chapter on National Income includes important terms like GDP, GNP, per capita income, and national output. Although it is theoretical, it carries high weightage. Students should understand the definitions and methods of calculating national income.

6. Market Structures

Another simple but important area is Market Structures, including perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly, and monopolistic competition. Understanding their features helps students attempt long questions with confidence.

Conclusion

To sum up, focusing on the main topics—basic concepts, opportunity cost, demand, supply, national income, and market structures—helps First Year Commerce students prepare more effectively for Karachi Board exams. With regular practice and clear notes, Economics becomes an easy and high-scoring subject.
